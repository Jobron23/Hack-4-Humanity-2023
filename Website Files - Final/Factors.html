Our Factors

pH Level

The pH scale is used to determine the acidity or the basicness of a solution. Values range from 0-14, with 7 being completely neutral. Values under 7 are considered acidic whereas those above 7 are considered basic. Although the pH of water is generally regarded as insignificant, pH values on the extreme ends of the spectrum are typically signs of water pollution. Avoid water that is too acidic, such as water with a pH value below 6.5. The safe range for drinking water is considered to be 6.5 to 8, however some waters labeled as “alkaline” often contain pH values of 9 and above.

Dissolved Oxygen Level

Put simply, dissolved oxygen is the measure of oxygen from the atmosphere that dissolves in water. Various things can have an effect on the level of oxygen that dissolves in the water. Plant population, debris, and water temperature are all factors that cause dissolved oxygen levels to vary. Additionally, depth has been found to play a role in levels. High dissolved levels play a crucial role in the survival of aquatic organisms. Like land animals, aquatic organisms still require oxygen to survive. When bacteria are present in water, they tend to consume a lot of oxygen. For there to be low oxygen levels, the rate at which bacteria consumes oxygen has to exceed that at which oxygen dissolves into the water. Therefore, dissolved oxygen levels can give us a lot of information about the quality of water and allow us to determine whether or not the water is safe to consume. 

Find more information here: USGS Website

Turbidity

Have you ever noticed that the water coming out of your faucet is slightly foggy? That water most likely has a high turbidity value. Turbidity is the measurement of liquid clarity. It’s a characteristic that is observable by the human eye, allowing us to easily determine the likelihood of water being safe to drink. The value of Turbidity is measured by collecting intensity of light being scattered through a liquid. The higher the intensity, the higher the turbidity. As turbidity increases, water begins to become cloudy and opaque.
